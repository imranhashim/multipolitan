05 Week-by-Week Execution Plan

Click each week to expand. Sequenced for maximum early impact. Perplexity wins first, ChatGPT follows, Gemini compounds.

1week
Audit, Baseline & Bot Access
GOAL: Establish ground truth before any optimisation
▾
  • Verify GPTBot, PerplexityBot, OAI-SearchBot are allowed in robots.txt
  • Manually test 15–20 target prompts across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude. Record: mentioned (Y/N), position, sentiment, source cited
  • Audit top 20 pages for LLMO-readiness: do they have direct-answer openers, question H-tags, and scannable structure?
  • Set up GA4 custom segment for referral traffic from openai.com, perplexity.ai, gemini.google.com
  • Identify top 50 high-intent prompts mapped to existing or needed pages
  • Add llms.txt to root directory

Deliverable

Baseline report: current citation rate, share of voice per platform, pages currently cited vs. not cited

Tracking Output

Week 0 snapshot across all KPIs. This is your proof of results at Day 60.

2week
Keyword Mapping & Entity Architecture
GOAL: Build the semantic map that guides all content work
▾
  • Map 5 keyword clusters (see Section 04) to specific existing pages: 1 cluster per page, no overlap
  • Define Multipolitan's entity description: "Multipolitan is a global mobility membership platform providing residency access, lifestyle benefits, and borderless living services." Use this verbatim on homepage, About page, meta descriptions, and first paragraph of all key pages
  • Identify content gaps: which queries cite competitors but not Multipolitan?
  • Build entity-relationship map: Brand → Programs → Countries → Benefits → Costs
  • Prioritise 10 pages for immediate on-page overhaul (Week 3)

Deliverable

Entity map + page-to-cluster assignment doc. Competitor citation analysis.

Tracking Output

Top 50 prioritised prompts locked. Gap analysis: which topics yield fastest wins.

3week
On-Page Architecture (Batch 1: Top 10 Pages)
GOAL: Make pages digestible for AI crawlers
▾
  • Answer-First: Add a 40–60 word direct answer block at the very top of each page, before any preamble
  • Question H-tags: Rewrite H2/H3 as natural-language questions ("What are the costs of the Malta CBI program in 2026?")
  • Schema: Implement FAQPage + Article schema on all 10 pages; Organization schema on homepage
  • Quick Facts box: Add bullet-point summary (Cost / Eligibility / Countries / Processing Time) early in each article
  • Paragraph chunking: Break any paragraph >3 sentences into 2–3 sentence chunks for AI parsing
  • Add 5–10 FAQ pairs per page (H3 question + 2–3 sentence direct answer)

Deliverable

10 pages live with new architecture. Schema validated via Google Rich Results Test.

Tracking Output

% of target pages with BLUF format. Schema pass rate (target 95%+).

4week
Comparison Content Sprint
GOAL: Own the decision-stage queries LLMs answer most often
▾
  • Create 3 comparison pages: (1) "Multipolitan vs Golden Visa", (2) "CBI Programs: Caribbean vs Europe Ranked", (3) "Digital Nomad Visa vs Residency by Investment"
  • Use side-by-side tables: Investment / Timeline / Visa-Free Travel / Tax Benefits / Eligibility
  • Add "Who should choose X" and "Who should choose Y" verdict sections as LLMs extract these verbatim
  • Publish 2 new "answer-first" articles targeting uncovered high-intent prompts from the gap analysis
  • Internal linking: link all new comparison pages back to a central "Global Mobility Guide" pillar page

Deliverable

3 comparison pages + 2 new articles live. Internal linking map updated.

Tracking Output

Re-run 15 target prompts. Expected: first mentions in Perplexity for comparison queries.

5week
On-Page Architecture (Batch 2: Next 15 Pages)
GOAL: Scale the Week 3 template across remaining priority pages
▾
  • Apply Week 3 optimisations to next 15 pages (service pages, country-specific pages, program guides)
  • Rewrite meta titles as intent-rich questions: "Malta CBI 2026: Costs, Timeline & How to Apply"
  • Ensure first 2 sentences of every page answer: What is it? Who is it for? Why does it matter?
  • Optimise image alt text with entity-rich descriptions (not keyword stuffing)
  • Add "In summary" / "Key Takeaways" section at bottom of each article
  • Add Author schema with credentials to establish E-E-A-T

Deliverable

25–30 pages total optimised. Meta title audit complete. Author schema live.

Tracking Output

Schema coverage (target 100%). Total optimised pages count.

6week
Freshness & Original Data
GOAL: Signal recency and primary-source authority to LLMs
▾
  • Update all pillar pages with 2026 data: new program costs, policy changes, visa-free travel numbers
  • Add original research hooks: "We analysed 50+ citizenship programs…" / "According to Multipolitan's 2026 Global Mobility Index…"
  • Display prominent "Last Updated: [date]" timestamps on all pages, make freshness visible
  • Set up weekly content audit protocol: top 10 pages refreshed every 2 weeks
  • Newsjack any recent visa policy changes (Portugal, Greece, UAE) as rapid-response articles within 48 hours of news
  • Add Review/Testimonial schema for case studies

Deliverable

All pillar pages updated. Freshness protocol documented. 1–2 newsjack articles published.

Tracking Output

Content freshness score (target: avg. <30 days since update). # original data points added.

7week
Internal Linking & Topical Authority
GOAL: Help LLMs understand your site hierarchy and topic ownership
▾
  • Audit internal links: ensure all optimised pages link to their cluster pillar page
  • Use entity-name anchor text (not just keywords): "global mobility membership" / "citizenship by investment programs"
  • Update "Resources" page linking to all pillar content
  • Ensure no optimised page is orphaned or behind login/paywall
  • Verify crawl depth: all key pages reachable within 3 clicks from homepage
  • Add "sameAs" schema links to verified social profiles and authoritative citations

Deliverable

Internal linking audit report with all fixes live. Knowledge Hub page published.

Tracking Output

Avg. internal links per page (target 4–6). Crawl depth ≤3 clicks for all key pages.

8week
Measure, Report & Iterate
GOAL: Prove ROI and set the roadmap for next 60 days
▾
  • Re-run the full Week 1 prompt test (same 15–20 prompts, same platforms). Compare results.
  • Pull GA4: referral traffic from AI domains, direct branded searches, CTR on optimised pages
  • Calculate AI Share of Voice: brand mentions ÷ total queries tested
  • Document wins: which pages are now cited, which keywords broke through first
  • Identify the next 10 prompt gaps to target in the following sprint
  • Compile 60-Day LLM Visibility Report with before/after metrics and ROI estimate

Deliverable

Full 60-Day report. Q2 LLMO backlog prioritised. Freshness calendar for ongoing maintenance.

Tracking Output

Final Share of Voice %, Citation Rate %, Branded Search Volume change, AI referral traffic.

07 Platform-Specific Tactics

Perplexity
Fastest return with real-time web retrieval. Changes can appear in citations within days of publishing. Target first. Prioritise freshness signals and structured data.
ChatGPT
Bing-indexed. Favours encyclopaedic, authoritative sources. Build Wikipedia-style comprehensive guides with citations. Expect 2–4 week lag after changes.
Google AI Overviews
Tied to traditional SEO + structured data. Maintaining Google rankings is prerequisite. Implement all schema types. Fastest to adopt FAQ schema.
Gemini
Closely tied to Google Search ranking. Strong E-E-A-T signals matter most. Author credentials and expert citations are critical differentiators.
